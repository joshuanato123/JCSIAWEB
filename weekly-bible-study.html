I Stand for Christ, I Support JCSIA

Faith is the foundation of my life, and I stand firmly for Christ in all that I do. Jesus Christ has given me purpose, strength, and an unwavering hope that guides my every step. In a world filled with uncertainties, I choose to anchor myself in His Word, living by faith and sharing His love with others.

This is why I fully support JCSIA (Jesus Christ Saves International Association). JCSIA is more than just an organization; it is a movement dedicated to spreading the gospel, strengthening believers, and serving communities with Christ’s love. Through discipleship, outreach, and unwavering commitment to biblical truth, JCSIA empowers individuals to live according to God’s purpose.

Supporting JCSIA means standing for righteousness, truth, and love. It means being part of a mission that seeks to transform lives through the power of Christ. As a believer, I am called to be a light in the darkness, a voice of hope, and a vessel for God’s work.

I will continue to stand for Christ and support JCSIA in its mission to bring souls to salvation. Together, we shine His light and proclaim His name to the ends of the earth.

Center for Biblical Studies

Center for Biblical Studies

Bible study is a spiritual practice that involves reading, interpreting, and applying Scripture to daily life. It helps believers grow in faith, understand God's will, and deepen their relationship with Him. Bible study can be done individually or in groups, using various methods such as verse-by-verse analysis, thematic studies, or character studies. Many use study guides, commentaries, and concordances to gain deeper insights. Churches often hold Bible study sessions to encourage fellowship and discussion. Personal study can include prayer and meditation on God's Word. Consistent Bible study strengthens faith, provides wisdom for life’s challenges, and fosters spiritual growth.

Lessons

LESSON #1: BEGINNING OF FAITH

Faith is the foundation of the Christian journey. It starts with trust in God, believing in His existence and His promises. The Bible teaches that without faith, it is impossible to please God. It’s through faith that we receive salvation and begin our relationship with God. As believers, we are called to walk by faith, not by sight, trusting in God's word and plan for our lives. This lesson helps to lay the groundwork for understanding the importance of faith as a crucial element in the Christian life.

LESSON #2: HOPE IN CHRIST

Hope in Christ is the confident expectation of eternal life and fulfillment of God's promises. It is not based on circumstances but on God's unchanging nature. Christians are encouraged to anchor their hope in the gospel, knowing that God is faithful and will fulfill His word. This hope leads to peace in times of difficulty and motivates believers to continue walking in the path God has set for them. Understanding this hope transforms how believers live their lives and impacts their relationship with others.

LESSON #3: LOVE YOUR NEIGHBOR

The commandment to love our neighbors is central to Jesus’ teachings. It calls believers to show kindness, empathy, and compassion to everyone around them, regardless of their background or status. Jesus demonstrated this love through His actions, even toward those who persecuted Him. This lesson emphasizes the importance of loving others as ourselves, as a reflection of God's love for us. It encourages Christians to be active in serving others and sharing the love of Christ with the world, promoting unity and peace.

LESSON #4: FORGIVENESS IN CHRIST

Forgiveness is a key principle in Christianity, exemplified by Jesus' sacrifice on the cross. Believers are called to forgive others as God forgives them. This lesson explores the power of forgiveness in healing relationships, restoring peace, and removing the burden of anger and resentment. Jesus’ teachings urge us to forgive seventy-seven times, signifying the endless nature of God’s grace. Forgiving others not only frees them but also releases us from the chains of unforgiveness, allowing us to experience true freedom in Christ.

LESSON #5: PRAYER AND COMMUNICATION WITH GOD

Prayer is essential for building a strong relationship with God. It allows believers to communicate with Him, express gratitude, seek guidance, and intercede for others. This lesson emphasizes the importance of regular prayer and trusting in God's responses, whether immediate or in His perfect timing.

LESSON #6: SERVING OTHERS

Jesus taught that serving others is a mark of greatness in God's kingdom. This lesson explores how believers can serve others selflessly, following Christ’s example. True service is an act of love, humility, and obedience to God's will, bringing blessings to both the giver and the receiver.

LESSON #7: THE POWER OF THE HOLY SPIRIT

The Holy Spirit empowers believers to live according to God’s will. This lesson discusses how the Spirit guides, comforts, and strengthens Christians in their daily walk. The gifts and fruits of the Spirit equip believers to serve God effectively and overcome spiritual challenges.

LESSON #8: LIVING A LIFE OF GRATITUDE

Gratitude is a powerful expression of faith and trust in God. This lesson highlights the importance of being thankful in all circumstances, recognizing God’s blessings, and developing a heart of worship. A grateful life leads to joy, peace, and a deeper connection with God.

Our Approach

Our Approach

Behold, I Make All Things New

We often say, “The Bible is a unified story that leads to Jesus.” This belief shapes how we engage with Scripture and influences how we create resources. Our approach is guided by seven key aspects of a paradigm that helps us explore, understand, and communicate the rich narrative of the Bible.

Human and Divine Literature

The Bible is the result of a creative collaboration between humans and God’s Spirit.

Ancient Literature

The Bible was written in another time and culture, and we need to honor that historical context.

Unified Literature

The Bible has many authors, styles, and themes but tells one story about God’s creation.

Messianic Literature

The story of the Bible comes to fulfillment in Jesus’ life, death, and resurrection.

Communal Literature

The Bible was designed to be read and studied within a community.

Wisdom Literature

The Bible invites readers to a journey of character transformation.

Meditation Literature

The Bible is designed to reveal its meaning over a lifetime of reflection.

Programs List

Our Programs

Adopt-a-Church Program

The Adopt-a-Church Program is a community-driven initiative that aims to support and restore churches in need. Through partnerships with individuals, organizations, and local businesses, the program provides financial assistance, volunteer services, and resources for renovations, maintenance, and outreach efforts, their churches thriving for future generations.

Bible Relevance in Modern Times

The Bible remains relevant today, offering wisdom, moral guidance, and hope in an ever-changing world. Its teachings on love, justice, and perseverance inspire individuals to navigate life’s challenges. From personal growth to societal ethics, the Bible continues to shape values, strengthen faith, and provide comfort in modern times.

Bible Museum

The Bible Museum showcases the history, impact, and significance of the Bible through interactive exhibits, ancient manuscripts, and archaeological artifacts. It educates visitors on biblical narratives, cultural influence, and historical preservation. The museum fosters appreciation for the Bible’s enduring legacy, making its message accessible to all generations.

Bible Listening Groups

Bible Listening Groups bring people together to hear and reflect on God’s Word. Using audio Bibles, these groups make Scripture accessible to all, especially those with literacy challenges. They foster spiritual growth, community bonding, and deeper understanding, helping individuals apply biblical teachings in daily life through shared discussions and faith-building.

Trauma Healing Program

A Trauma Healing Program provides emotional and spiritual support to individuals recovering from pain and loss. Combining biblical principles with counseling, it helps participants process grief, find hope, and rebuild resilience. Through guided discussions, prayer, and community support, the program fosters healing and restoration for those facing life’s deepest wounds.

Salvation Through Christ

Salvation through Christ is God’s greatest gift, offering eternal life to all who believe in Jesus. Through His sacrificial death on the cross, sins are completely forgiven, and believers are fully reconciled with God. By accepting Christ in faith and repentance, individuals receive divine grace, unshakable hope, spiritual renewal, and the promise of everlasting life in God’s glorious presence. This salvation transforms lives, bringing peace, joy, and purpose. It is not earned by works but given freely through God’s boundless love and mercy, inviting everyone to experience true redemption and a personal relationship with Him.

Personal Revelation

Reading the Bible

When I read the Bible, I often have many revelations and feel enlightened. I began sharing these thoughts with a small group of Chinese Christians and later began to share them over the internet, which helped many people. I did not realize that it was the Holy Spirit speaking to me until I heard Mark Virkler teach that God speaks to us through "spontaneous thought." There are many messages from this group in Chinese, and I hope to share them in English gradually.

Even though my ministry focuses on Chinese, I felt I should start a website in English where I could share what the Lord is doing among Chinese people. If you are interested but don’t understand Chinese or have the opportunity to interact with Chinese Christians, you can pray for us or support us financially. I believe it will be beneficial to share what I have received from the Lord.
