About - PhD in Spirituality

About

The Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) in Spirituality degree serves the church and society by educating doctoral-level scholars in the areas of prayer, mysticism, spiritual discernment, and spiritual guidance through a rich and rigorous program.

Work with renowned, influential faculty who bring expertise in a wide range of disciplines within Christian Spirituality, engage in richly ecumenical dialogue and innovative scholarship and prepare to lead in your field.

This is the only fully-funded doctoral program in Christian Spirituality in the United States. Our faculty comprises internationally-recognized scholars and leaders in the field of spirituality.

Program Highlights

Course Description

The Bachelor of Arts in Theology provides a deep exploration of religious traditions, scripture, ethics, and theological thought. It equips students with critical thinking, interpretive, and pastoral skills, fostering a profound understanding of faith and its role in society. This program prepares graduates for ministry, education, counseling, and community leadership.

  • Scriptural Studies – In-depth analysis of religious texts, their historical contexts, and interpretations.
  • Ethics and Moral Theology – Examination of ethical principles, moral dilemmas, and their application in contemporary society.
  • Pastoral and Ministerial Training – Development of leadership, counseling, and community service skills for ministry and outreach.
  • Comparative Theology – Study of different religious traditions and their impact on culture, society, and interfaith dialogue.

Learning outcomes

Develop theological knowledge, ethical reasoning, pastoral skills, critical thinking, interfaith dialogue, and community leadership for faith-based service and education.

  • Demonstrate a comprehensive understanding of biblical and theological concepts.
  • Analyze historical and contemporary religious traditions.
  • Interpret sacred texts using scholarly methodologies.
  • Apply theological principles to ethical and moral decision-making.
  • Communicate theological ideas effectively in written and oral formats.
  • Develop leadership skills for ministry and community engagement.
  • Understand and articulate different theological perspectives.
  • Engage in interfaith and ecumenical dialogue.
  • Integrate theology with contemporary social issues.
  • Exhibit critical thinking and problem-solving skills in theological discourse.
  • Conduct theological research using appropriate academic resources.
  • Demonstrate cultural sensitivity in religious and spiritual contexts.
  • Apply theological knowledge in pastoral care and counseling.
  • Engage with philosophical and ethical dimensions of theology.
  • Participate in service and mission-oriented activities.
  • Understand the role of theology in public life and policy.
  • Develop skills in sermon preparation and religious education.

Vocation and Career Opportunities

Upon completing the Bachelor of Arts in Theology, Major in Christian Education, students will have developed:

  • Christian Counselor

  • Social Justice Advocate

  • Theology Librarian

Program Highlights

This program emphasizes scriptural studies, historical theology, ethics, and pastoral care. Students engage in theological discourse, critical analysis, and practical ministry training. Opportunities include internships, research projects, and interfaith studies. The curriculum fosters spiritual growth, leadership development, and cross-cultural engagement, preparing students for diverse roles in religious and community settings.

  • Comprehensive Scriptural Studies – In-depth exploration of biblical texts and interpretations.
  • Historical and Systematic Theology – Study of theological traditions and their evolution.
  • Ethics and Moral Theology – Application of ethical principles in faith and society.
  • Pastoral Care and Counseling – Training in spiritual guidance and community support.
  • Critical Theological Discourse – Development of analytical and interpretative skills.
  • Interfaith and Ecumenical Studies – Engagement with diverse religious traditions.
  • Practical Ministry Training – Hands-on experience through internships and outreach.
  • Leadership and Spiritual Formation – Preparation for church and community leadership.
  • Research and Theological Scholarship – Conducting academic studies and theological inquiries.
  • Cross-Cultural and Missionary Engagement – Understanding theology in global and cultural contexts.
