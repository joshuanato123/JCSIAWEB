History

JCSIA HISTORY

Jesus Christ the Savior International Assemblies (JCSIA) is a full Gospel non- Sectarian church. Where its Doctrines adhere the Assembly of God (AG) world wide. JCSIA is now 33 Years in existence and started its name Asian Christian Charismatic Fellowship(ACCF) under Paul Clark the American Missionary with the Cooperation of Jimmy Swaggart Ministry. The church started with a little Bible Study in Sabarte quezon city and extended to Pangarap Caloocan City. By that time Romeo Analupa as leader of the Bible Study in Pangarap is connected to Jaren Lapasaran a Senior Ptr of ACCF main in Cubao. During this time Jimmy Swaggart missionaries have come to help pentecostal churches in the Philippines, they have conducted crusades and house to house evangelism in different parts of Metro Manila and nearby provinces. These missionaries have done astounding efforts they are able to win many people for Christ. After evangelisn they bought 10 Big lots for churches in Metro Manila and one of the recipients is JCSIA which is located in Bankers Caloocan City. The church started a little member at approximately 50, Ptr Romeo Analupa carried its vision to increase the members of the church therefore he conducted many evangelistic crusades in Quezon City, Caloocan, and Bulacan after eight years of handling ACCF Bankers, he has a new leading of God and he transferred the leadership of the church to his assistant Romeo Anasco.

ACCF TO JCSIA

The Church has now a new leader Rome Anasco, from the Province of Mindanao, Anasco earned his bachelor degree in Christian education at Four Square Bible College. He leads the church for 13 years during his time Mighty Men sprouted these are group of young people in the church who become pillars in prayer and evangelism. Mighty Men is led by Ptr Dong Dotosme the assistant Ptr of Rome Anasco. From this group of young generation of mighty men, the church of JCSIA exploded. By this time Ptr Romeo Anasco is led by the Holy Spirit to change the name of the church from ACCF to JCSIA due to the fact that ACCF churches are dwindling for leadership triumviri. Ptr Jaren the leader of ACCF main goes to USA as a missionary, Bong Valdez and Rick Oasin remain as leader of ACCF and Romeo Anasco change the name of ACCF Bankers to JCSIA as per advice by Ptr Jaren Lapasaran and help of the elders of the local church. After 13 Years of being a Ptr of JCSIA Romeo Anasco transferred his leadership to Derio Dotosme who happens to be his assistant during his leadership, as help by the leadership of Southern Tagalog District Council of the Assembly of God (STDC AG).

Introduction

Hello, my name is Pastor Derio Dotosme, with Jesus Christ the Savior International Assemblies, in Metro Manila, Philippines. I grew up in Masbate Dimasalang in the Bicol Region on Masbate, Island, the youngest of thirteen children. My dad was a poor farmer and an Albularyo (Filipino term for a witch doctor, folk healer, or medicine man). Mom took care of all the kids and did not participate in witchcraft.

Childhood and Spiritual Awakening

Growing up, we were not involved with a church, and I didn’t even know what a Bible was. My father had strange powers that I didn’t understand. Fishermen often called on him to ensure their fishing trips ended with a full boat of fish. He also helped people by breaking spells, receiving money for his services.

At Easter time, for 21 days, my father would retreat to different locations for fasting and battling demons. He started in a cave, then a cemetery, and finally a church. He never took any of us with him. He died when I was eleven, and our family moved to Metro Manila.

Finding Jesus

In Manila, my brother got a job as a dishwasher and met Christians who gave him a tract about Jesus. He invited me to church, and after two years, I finally accepted Jesus as my Lord and Savior when I learned about His love in John 3:16.

We then moved to another church that fit my work schedule. Here, I experienced deliverance. The Pastor prayed for me, and huge burdens were lifted. One by one, my entire family, including extended relatives, found Jesus. It was a revival.

God’s Calling and Ministry

After high school, I worked in a factory but had a dream where Jesus called me to preach. I resisted, saying I was too shy. But I knew I had to go to Bible school. Before enrolling, I went on a one-year mission, spending a month with each of twelve mountain tribes, learning evangelism.

When I returned, I became a barber by day and studied the Bible during slow hours. After seven years, I graduated from Bible school. We started a training program, and soon we were opening new churches every month. Today, we have:

  • Graduated 60 pastors
  • Opened 32 churches (extending to Korea and Bahrain)
  • Sponsored 230 children for education
Family and Future Vision

I met Maria Louisa in Bible school, and after three years of praying together, we got married. We now serve in ministry together. Our vision for the future is to build a sanctuary for 10,000 people and continue planting churches.

The Meaning of the Cross

The Cross is everything to me. Without Jesus’ sacrifice, I would not have eternal life. The Cross planted at our church is a testament to our faith, declaring that we believe in Jesus Christ.

Prayer

Father, thank You for establishing visions, dreams, and opening doors for Your kingdom. In the name of the Lord Jesus Christ. Amen!
